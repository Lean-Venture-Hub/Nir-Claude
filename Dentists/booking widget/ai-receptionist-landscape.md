# AI Receptionist & Booking Assistant Landscape: Dental + Med Spa
*Last updated: 2026-03-05*

## TL;DR

The AI receptionist space for dental and med spa has exploded since 2024, with 20+ companies now competing. The market splits into four tiers: (1) dental-specific AI receptionists (Arini, HeyGent, Viva AI, Dentina, Rondah, Zaha, Flossy, TensorLinks, Annie, Resonate) handling phone calls and SMS; (2) med spa-specific AI (Eva AI, MySalonDesk, AvraVoice, BookingBee, Spa Voices); (3) general AI phone/voice platforms used across healthcare (Smith.ai, TrueLark, Dialzara, Synthflow, Goodcall, My AI Front Desk); and (4) infrastructure platforms that agencies build on (Vapi, Bland AI, PolyAI). Pricing ranges from $45/mo (My AI Front Desk) to $500+/mo (TrueLark, Arini for DSOs). Nearly all focus on phone call answering -- the website chat booking widget space remains almost empty.

Our widget occupies a fundamentally different lane. While every competitor is building an AI that answers phone calls (replacing the receptionist on calls), we are building an AI that lives on the website and books appointments through chat -- replacing the need for the phone call entirely. At $99-149/mo with an embeddable script tag, we sit below the $200-500/mo AI phone receptionist tier and address the actual patient behavior shift: 69% of patients prefer online booking over calling.

**TrueLark** -- Multi-industry AI receptionist with strong dental vertical. From $345/mo. Good for DSOs/DPOs needing centralized comms. Reviews note AI sometimes books wrong slot durations. Established player, less dental-native feel.

**Flossy (Fiona)** -- AI receptionist that handles phone, SMS, and web chat. Claims to have passed dental board exam. Multilingual. Positioning as "most affordable" but no public pricing. Web chat capability makes it closest to our concept.

**Pearl** -- Not a receptionist. FDA-cleared dental AI for X-ray analysis and practice intelligence. Added scheduling optimization features. $30K/mo production boost claim. Different lane (clinical AI).

**Resonate AI** -- SMS-based missed call follow-up rather than live call answering. Texts missed callers, engages in AI conversation to book. Claims 174+ new patients/mo and 60x ROI. Interesting "text-back" model.

## What They All Get Wrong (Gaps and Opportunities)

1. **Phone-call obsession:** Nearly every player is building AI to answer phone calls. But 69% of patients prefer online booking. The phone call is the problem, not the solution -- patients want to avoid calling.

2. **No lightweight website widget:** None offer a simple embeddable chat widget for the clinic's website. HeyGent and Flossy mention "web chat" but it's part of their $300+/mo full-stack solution, not a standalone affordable widget.

3. **Pricing floor is too high:** Most dental AI receptionists start at $200-500/mo. Solo practitioners and small clinics (the majority of the market) can't justify this when their current solution is "the front desk person who also answers phones."

4. **Overkill for simple needs:** Practices that just want a way for website visitors to book an appointment get sold a full phone system replacement. Many don't need AI to answer calls -- they need AI to prevent the call from being necessary.

5. **Setup friction:** Most require PMS integration, phone system changes, or lengthy onboarding. No one offers a "paste this script tag and go live in 15 minutes" experience.

6. **No-show gap:** Very few tie booking to deposits or prepayment. They book appointments but don't solve the $20-70K/yr no-show problem.

7. **Chat-to-book conversion on the website is ignored:** When a patient lands on a dental website at 9pm, their options are: (a) fill out a contact form and wait, (b) call during business hours tomorrow, or (c) leave. Nobody is catching these visitors with an instant conversational booking experience.
