# Monday.com Integration Agencies: Market Research
*Last updated: February 2026*

## TL;DR

Monday.com crossed $1.23B in revenue (FY2025) with 225,000+ customers globally, and its partner ecosystem of ~2,000 partners now drives ~20% of gross added ARR. The market for Monday.com implementation services is a real but fragmented cottage industry — primarily small boutique agencies charging $3K–$50K per project, competing on niche expertise (CRM, marketing ops, construction, etc.) and geography. Growth is accelerating as Monday.com deliberately pushes enterprise deals through partners, opening the door for agencies that can combine technical integrations with change management.

For SMBs specifically, the serviceable market is smaller than it looks: most SMB implementations are $3K–$15K one-time, with optional retainers at $1K–$3K/month. Agencies are increasingly building recurring revenue through ongoing optimization retainers, custom app development, and Zapier/Make automation buildouts rather than pure implementation.

---

## 1. Market Overview

**What is this market?**
Agencies that sell, implement, customize, and integrate Monday.com for business clients. Work ranges from basic board setup and training to complex multi-system API integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot, ERP systems), custom app development, and ongoing optimization retainers.

**Monday.com Partner Ecosystem Structure**

| Program Type | Description | Revenue Model |
|---|---|---|
| Solution Partners | Refer, resell, and implement Monday.com | Commission (10–35%) + services revenue |
| Service Partners | Deliver professional services only | Services fees only |
| Marketplace Partners | Build and sell apps on Monday.com App Marketplace | Revenue share from app sales |
| Affiliate Partners | Pure referral/lead generation | 15% ongoing commission |

- Partners: ~2,000 globally (270+ solution partners, 600+ marketplace app developers)
- Certification: Free to join; Silver / Gold / Platinum tiers
- MDF (market development funds) provided to higher-tier partners
- Partners contribute **23% of Monday.com's gross added ARR** (as of early 2026)
- Competitor Atlassian generates 50%+ of revenue through partners — Monday.com has room to grow significantly

## 4. Pricing & Ticket Size

### Implementation Project Pricing
| Scope | Target | Est. Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Basic setup (boards, templates, training) | SMB <20 users | $500–$3,000 |
| Standard implementation (workflows, automations) | SMB 20–50 users | $3,000–$10,000 |
| Multi-system integration (CRM/ERP/Slack etc.) | SMB/Mid-market | $10,000–$30,000 |
| Complex enterprise deployment | Enterprise | $30,000–$100,000+ |

### Typical SMB Project Breakdown (50-person company)
- Implementation services: $10,000–$15,000
- Data migration: $3,000–$7,000
- Training program: $2,000–$4,000
- Integration development: $3,000–$8,000
- **Total project: ~$18,000–$34,000**

### Ongoing / Retainer Pricing
| Service | Est. Monthly Cost |
|---|---|
| Light support + optimization (5–10 hrs/month) | $750–$1,500 |
| Active admin + automation management | $1,500–$3,500 |
| Embedded ops team (strategic + technical) | $3,500–$8,000+ |

### Agency Hourly Rates (Est.)
- Boutique SMB-focused agencies: $75–$125/hour
- Mid-size agencies: $100–$175/hour
- Enterprise/specialty firms: $150–$250/hour

### SMB vs. Enterprise Pricing Differences
- SMBs: Fixed-scope packages, templated solutions, faster delivery (1–4 weeks)
- Enterprise: Time-and-materials or milestone billing, 8–26 week engagements, governance/change mgmt included

## 6. Competitive Landscape

**How agencies differentiate:**
- **Vertical niche**: Construction (BuildOps), marketing agencies, professional services, healthcare
- **Geography**: EU-focused (Deviniti), ANZ (Fruition), India (Transfunnel), US boutiques
- **Technical depth**: Some focus on API-heavy integrations + custom apps; others stay surface-level
- **Speed-to-value**: "8-week implementation" promises vs. longer enterprise engagements
- **Migration specialization**: Targeting companies switching from Asana, ClickUp, Jira
- **Certification tier**: Platinum status is a trust signal for mid-market buyers

**Key competitive dynamics:**
- Highly fragmented — no dominant player in SMB segment
- Most agencies are generalist with Monday.com as one of several tools (also offer HubSpot, Salesforce, Jira consulting)
- Monday.com itself offers "monday.com sales team" for enterprise, creating mild channel conflict
- Differentiation via thought leadership (YouTube tutorials, templates) is a common CAC strategy

## 8. Revenue Models for Agencies

| Revenue Stream | Description | % of Agency Revenue (Est.) |
|---|---|---|
| Project implementation fees | One-time setup/integration projects | 50–65% |
| Monthly retainers | Ongoing support, optimization, admin | 20–35% |
| License commission | 10–35% referral fee on new Monday.com licenses | 5–15% |
| Custom app development | Build apps for Monday.com Marketplace | 5–10% (tech-heavy agencies) |
| Training workshops | Group or 1:1 training sessions | 3–8% |

**Key insight:** The most profitable agencies are building predictable MRR through retainers, since project work alone creates revenue volatility. Top agencies are also developing proprietary Monday.com apps (one-time build, recurring marketplace revenue) as a scalable income stream.
