# Med Spas as Next Vertical: City-Level Deep Dive

## TL;DR

The US med spa market (~$7-8B in 2025, ~11,500 locations, 14% CAGR) is a high-growth SMB vertical with strong parallels to dentists but higher average revenue ($1.9M/yr vs ~$800K for solo dental). Critically, 63% of med spas are owned by non-physicians (investors, NPs, business operators) who are more marketing-savvy but also more desperate for differentiation in an increasingly saturated market. The digital gaps are enormous: only 18% use marketing automation, only 35% use a CRM, and most have outdated websites with poor SEO while over-investing in Instagram.

**Recommended pilot: Dallas-Fort Worth, Tampa, and Nashville.** DFW offers the largest addressable market in a business-friendly state with flexible ownership laws. Tampa combines strong demographics, Florida's open ownership rules, and lower agency competition. Nashville is a fast-growing, affluent metro with moderate saturation and favorable Tennessee regulations. Scan 150 med spas across these 3 cities as a first batch.

### How They Acquire Customers Today

1. **Instagram/TikTok** -- most over-invest here relative to ROI
2. **Google Ads / Meta Ads** -- primary paid channels ($16-27 cost per lead)
3. **Referrals / word-of-mouth** -- still #1 for established spas
4. **Google organic / local SEO** -- underinvested despite 80%+ of patients starting on Google
5. **RealSelf** -- declining relevance, rebranded Sept 2025
6. **Groupon** -- used for volume but attracts deal-seekers, low LTV

### Key Pain Points (Our Opportunity)

- Only **18% use marketing automation**; 35% use a CRM
- Websites are outdated, rarely updated, poor mobile experience
- Fragmented brand: different tone on website vs social vs booking platform
- Over-reliance on social media, neglecting SEO and Google Business Profile
- No structured review management or reputation monitoring
- Booking friction: many still use phone or generic forms
- HIPAA compliance anxiety around digital marketing

## 2. State Regulatory Comparison (Our 9 States)

| State | Who Can Own | Supervision | Ease Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Florida** | Anyone | Licensed professionals perform services | Easy |
| **Arizona** | Anyone | Truthful advertising required | Easy |
| **Texas** | Physician required (except laser hair removal) | Physician must supervise | Moderate |
| **North Carolina** | Non-physicians OK | Medical director required | Moderate |
| **Tennessee** | Physicians + certain licensed pros | Truthful advertising | Moderate |
| **Nevada** | Physician ownership | Profit-sharing allowed for non-MDs | Moderate |
| **Colorado** | Physician required | Physician oversight | Strict |
| **New York** | Physicians, NPs, physician groups | Truthful advertising | Strict |
| **Georgia** | Physicians only | Physician ownership mandated | Strict |

**Implication:** FL and AZ have the most open ownership = more med spas per capita = larger addressable market but also more competition. GA, NY, and CO have restrictive ownership = fewer but more established (physician-run) med spas.

### Ranking Rationale

**DFW (#1):** Largest TX metro, massive sprawl with hundreds of med spas, high growth, business-friendly culture. TX physician-ownership requirement actually helps us -- physician-owners are busier and more likely to need our services. Three distinct sub-markets (Dallas, Fort Worth, Arlington).

**Tampa (#2):** FL's open ownership laws mean lots of NP/investor-owned spas that need marketing help. Strong retiree + young professional demographics. Less agency competition than Miami. Medical tourism angle.

**Nashville (#3):** Fastest-growing city on our list by population. Booming aesthetics market driven by entertainment/image culture. Lower saturation = less noise for our outreach. Moderate regulations.

**NYC (#11):** Despite huge market, extreme saturation, high competition from established agencies, strict NY regulations, and high cost of client acquisition make it the worst pilot market. Save for scale phase.

## 5. Value Proposition & Scanning Strategy

### What We Can Scan (Public Data)

- Google Business Profile: ratings, review count, response rate, photos, hours, categories
- Website: mobile-friendliness, load speed, SSL, booking integration, content freshness
- Yelp: reviews, response rate, photos
- RealSelf: profile presence, reviews, before/after photos
- Instagram: follower count, posting frequency, engagement
- Online booking: present/absent, platform used (Calendly, Jane, custom, etc.)

### Med Spa-Specific Audit Gaps (Different from Dentists)

| Audit Point | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Before/after gallery quality | #1 conversion factor for aesthetics |
| Instagram integration on website | Social proof is critical for this vertical |
| Online booking availability | Many still use phone-only |
| Service page depth | Thin "menu" pages vs. rich procedure pages |
| Pricing transparency | Patients expect at least ranges |
| Review volume + recency | Need 50+ reviews and recent ones |
| Google Business Profile optimization | Most have incomplete profiles |
| Mobile experience | 70%+ of aesthetics searches are mobile |
| HIPAA-compliant forms | Many use non-compliant contact forms |
| Membership/loyalty program visibility | 24% uptick in membership sales |

### Our Offer Stack (Med Spa Version)

1. **Modern website** with before/after galleries, service pages, provider bios
2. **AI assistant** for treatment Q&A, pre-consultation screening, appointment booking
3. **Online booking integration** (reduce phone dependency)
4. **Review management** (automated ask + response)
5. **Local SEO optimization** (GBP, directories, RealSelf)
6. **Membership program** landing page and promotion

### Sales Pitch Differences vs. Dentists

| Dentists | Med Spas |
|---|---|
| "Patients can't find you" | "Your website doesn't match your Instagram" |
| Insurance-driven, price-sensitive | Cash-pay, premium positioning |
| Clinical trust = key | Visual proof (B&A photos) = key |
| Steady demand | Seasonal + trend-driven demand |
| Conservative buyers | More marketing-aware, faster decision-makers |

## 6. Recommended Pilot Plan

### Phase 1: Scan (Weeks 1-2)
- **DFW:** Scan 60 med spas
- **Tampa:** Scan 50 med spas
- **Nashville:** Scan 40 med spas
- Total: 150 med spas

### Phase 2: Outreach (Weeks 3-4)
- Personalized audit reports (adapted from dentist template)
- Email + Instagram DM outreach (med spas check DMs more than email)
- Target: 10-15% response rate

### Phase 3: Evaluate (Week 5)
- Compare response rates, deal sizes, and close rates vs. dentist vertical
- Decision: scale to remaining 8 cities or pivot approach

### Key Metrics to Track
- Response rate vs. dentist baseline
- Average deal size (target: $1,500-2,500/mo given higher revenue)
- Sales cycle length
- Most common objection themes
