# Landscaping — Vertical Website Guide
**Date:** 2026-03-13 | **Market:** US | **Purpose:** Template builder reference

## TL;DR
$153B industry where homeowners buy peace of mind, not grass cuts. 85% trust reviews like referrals; 77% rely on word-of-mouth. Primary fear: "cowboy contractor." Winning sites: cinematic visuals (before/after sliders, drone video), frictionless conversion (sticky CTA, 5-field forms, click-to-call), aggressive trust layering (Google stars above fold, crew photos, license badges). Before/after slider is the single highest-impact element. 80% of leads come via phone — click-to-call is non-negotiable.

## Customer Psychology

### Top Fears
1. **Cowboy contractor** — deposit paid, shoddy work or vanishes
2. **Budget blowouts** — hidden costs, final bill >> estimate
3. **Property damage** — existing plants destroyed, home exterior damaged
4. **Unsatisfactory result** — contractor doesn't listen to vision
5. **Chemical phobia** — pesticides harming pets/kids/biodiversity

### Trust Signals (by impact)
**Tier 1 — Must have:** Google reviews (85% trust like referrals), real crew photos, license/insurance badges, before/after portfolio, transparent pricing
**Tier 2 — Strong:** Awards/certifications, years in business, project count, warranty info
**Tier 3 — Nice to have:** Industry association logos, eco-certifications, video testimonials

### Decision Drivers
1. Speed of response (76% report labor shortages — who answers wins)
2. Listening/consultation style (88% want to improve how they *use* space)
3. Low-maintenance sustainability (native plants, drought-resistant)
4. ROI framing (5.5-12.7% property value increase)

---

## Must-Have Sections (Priority Order)
1. Hero (photo/video + headline + 2 CTAs + phone)
2. Trust bar (stars + years + project count + awards)
3. Services overview (3-6 cards linking to service pages)
4. Before/after slider (2-3 examples)
5. About (real crew photo, local story, family-owned)
6. Portfolio gallery (filterable by type)
7. Testimonials (distributed, not siloed)
8. Process ("How It Works" — 3 steps)
9. Service area map
10. Footer CTA (estimate form)

## Visual Direction

### Color Palettes
| Palette | When to Use |
|---------|-------------|
| Forest green + white + orange CTA | Default — nature + high-contrast conversion |
| Charcoal/slate + white + gold | Premium/luxury positioning |
| Olive/sage + terracotta + cream | Warm family-owned feel |
| Navy + green + white | Ecological/scientific positioning |
| Deep green + cream + copper | Editorial/sophisticated |

### Hero Patterns
1. Full-bleed project photo + dark gradient + headline + 2 CTAs
2. Autoplay drone video loop (silent, <5MB)
3. Split-screen: brand block left, project photo right
4. Full-screen video with floating trust signals

### Photography Rules
- Real over stock (homeowners distrust generic)
- Before/after slider = strongest selling tool
- Show humans (crew in branded uniforms)
- Golden hour shots (sunrise/sunset)
- Aerial/drone shots signal scale
- Climate-accurate (no bluegrass in Arizona)

## Anti-Patterns
- Hidden contact info / phone number buried
- Stock photo overuse (destroys local trust)
- Wall of text (homeowners scan)
- Stale site (2019 copyright = looks closed)
- No mobile optimization (60%+ search on mobile)
- Generic homepage for all searches (use service-specific pages)
- Budget field on forms (kills conversions)
- "Submit" button (use action-driven copy)
- No trust signals near forms
- No before/after content (landscaping is a visual sale)
