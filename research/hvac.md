# HVAC Vertical Research — Website Best Practices

## TL;DR

HVAC is a **distress-purchase industry** — most customers contact you when they're already panicked (broken AC in July, dead furnace in January). This means the entire website must be optimized for speed-to-contact, trust, and reducing anxiety rather than educating. The top conversion drivers are: visible phone number, real team photos, upfront pricing signals, and social proof above the fold. Local SEO with city/service landing pages dominates organic acquisition.

## 2. Customer Psychology

### Top Fears
| Fear | Frequency | Design Response |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Getting ripped off / upselling | Very High | Upfront pricing page, flat-rate repair language, no-surprise guarantee |
| Emergency breakdown (heat/AC dies) | Very High | Sticky "Call Now" 24/7 banner, same-day dispatch promise |
| Hidden costs / surprise bill | High | "No hidden fees" in hero subheadline, diagnostic fee policy stated |
| Incompetent or unlicensed techs | High | NATE certification, license numbers, background-check badges |
| Stranger in the home | High | Real tech photos with names, uniform/bootie policy mentioned |
| System too expensive to replace | High | "Financing as low as $X/mo" displayed next to CTAs |

### Trust Signals (above the fold)
- NATE Certified badge
- Carrier / Trane / Lennox / Rheem Factory Authorized Dealer
- BBB A+ rating with clickable logo
- Google Review count + stars (embed live widget)
- "Licensed & Insured" + license number in footer
- Years in business ("Serving [City] since 1998")
- Background-checked technicians badge

### Decision Drivers
1. **Speed** — "Available today," "2-hour window," "24/7 emergency"
2. **Transparent pricing** — flat-rate, upfront estimates, no overtime fees stated
3. **Warranties** — equipment warranty (10 yr parts), labor warranty (1 yr), satisfaction guarantee
4. **Financing** — "0% for 18 months" removes purchase barrier immediately
5. **Reviews** — volume matters (1,000+ reviews beats 4.9 stars with 12 reviews)

## 5. Visual Direction

### Color Palettes

| Palette | Primary | Secondary | Accent (CTAs) | Best For |
|---------|---------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| Industry Standard | Navy Blue | Ice Blue / Cyan | Orange or Red | Most residential HVAC |
| Eco/Efficiency | Forest Green | Slate Gray | Gold / Yellow | Energy-efficient, heat pump focus |
| Premium/Commercial | Charcoal / Black | Metallic Silver | Electric Blue | Commercial, high-end residential |
| Friendly Local | Sky Blue | White | Warm Orange | Family-owned, community feel |

### Hero Section Formula
- **H1:** Localized + benefit-driven: "Fast, Honest AC Repair in [City]"
- **Subheadline:** Address pain: "Licensed techs at your door in 2 hours. No hidden fees. Guaranteed."
- **Primary CTA:** High-contrast button — "Book Service Online" or "Call Now"
- **Secondary CTA:** Large bold phone number top-right
- **Social proof chip:** "Trusted by 3,000+ [City] homeowners | 4.9 ★ Google"
- **Background:** Real photo of branded truck + smiling tech, OR full-screen video loop of actual job site

### Photography Rules
1. Real team photos always beat stock — branded van in a driveway beats studio model
2. Show technicians: clean uniform, shoe booties on, smiling = psychological safety
3. Show the outcome: family comfortable at home > close-up of equipment
4. Before/after shots belong in a dedicated "Our Work" section, not the hero
5. Avoid: model-with-wrench-fake-smile, dirty backgrounds, unbranded work vans
6. Videos: employee intro clips and job-site walkthroughs outperform all static photos

### Typography
- Sans-serif: Inter, Montserrat, Roboto (readable at all sizes for emergency users)
- Bold headline weight (700-900) for phone numbers and CTAs
- Body copy at 16-18px minimum for mobile

## 6. Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Phone number not in header | Emergency customers leave immediately | Sticky header with clickable phone at all times |
| Long contact form (6+ fields) | Friction kills conversions | Name + Phone + "What's the problem?" only |
| Stock photos of models with wrenches | Destroys trust, screams fake | Real team photos, even low-quality iPhone shots |
| Jargon overload (SEER2, BTU, R-410A) | Customers don't care, they want comfort | Sell benefits ("whisper-quiet, lower bills") not specs |
| Ghost town blog (last post 2021) | Signals possibly out of business | Remove the blog or redirect to FAQ/service pages |
| No emergency/24-7 callout | Leaves money on the table | Banner or badge visible on every page |
| Slow mobile load (>3s) | 60-70% of HVAC searches are mobile | Compress images, lazy-load video, core web vitals |
| Generic headline ("Welcome to Our Website") | Zero differentiation | City name + benefit + speed/trust in first 5 words |
| No financing mention | Sticker shock causes abandonment | "Financing available" near every service CTA |
| Single "Services" page | Kills local SEO | Separate URL per service + per city combo |
| No license number visible | Legally suspicious in many states | License # in footer, "Licensed & Insured" in hero |

## So What? (Business Implications)

- **Template priority:** HVAC sites need an emergency-mode design variant — sticky header, phone-first, minimal scroll before CTA
- **Key differentiator to bake into templates:** Financing callout and guarantee badge should be near every service CTA, not just footer
- **Photography gap:** Most small HVAC clients won't have real photos — template should be designed to work with branded truck photos (common) even without team shots
- **SEO structure:** Templates should scaffold one URL per service + location combo (AC repair Austin, furnace repair Austin) — not a single services page
- **Trust bar:** Include a "trust bar" component (NATE / BBB / Google Stars / Licensed & Insured) directly below the hero — standard pattern in the vertical
- **Contradiction flagged:** Some sources recommend heavy video (engagement) while others warn against it (slow mobile load). Resolution: use poster image with lazy-loaded video, never autoplay on mobile
