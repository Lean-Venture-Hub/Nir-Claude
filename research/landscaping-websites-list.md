# Landscaping Websites Research: Design Examples & Best Practices

**TL;DR:** The best landscaping websites win on three axes — cinematic visuals (real project photography, video heroes, before/after sliders), frictionless conversion (sticky CTAs, simple 5-field forms, click-to-call), and aggressive trust layering (Google stars above the fold, awards, crew photos). 73% of landscaping customers research a website before calling. 80% of leads arrive via phone — so click-to-call is non-negotiable. Average conversion rate is 1–3%; service-specific landing pages push this to 5–15%.

## Part 3: Design Patterns (Observed Across Top Sites)

### Color Palettes
| Pattern | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Dominant approach | Forest/dark green primary + white/off-white background + high-contrast orange or yellow CTA |
| Premium approach | Deep green or charcoal + white + gold accent (signals luxury pricing) |
| Earthy approach | Olive/sage + terracotta/brown + cream (warm, family-owned feel) |
| Navy variant | Navy + green + white (Native Landscape Design) — feels ecological/scientific |
| Break-the-mold | Avoid green entirely with vibrant geometric branding (Martha Schwartz Partners) — only works for avant-garde firms |

### Hero Section Styles (ranked by frequency)
1. Full-bleed project photo with dark gradient overlay + headline + 2 CTAs
2. Autoplay looping video (drone or property walkthrough, silent, <5MB)
3. Split-screen: brand color block left, project photo right
4. Full-screen video with floating trust signals (awards, Google stars)

### Standard Page/Section Order (high-converting pattern)
1. Hero (photo/video + headline + 2 CTAs + phone)
2. Trust bar (Google stars + years in business + number of projects + awards)
3. Services overview (3–6 cards, each linking to dedicated service page)
4. Before/after slider (2–3 examples)
5. About section (real owner/crew photo, local story, family-owned flag)
6. Portfolio/gallery (filterable by service type)
7. Testimonials (distributed across page, not siloed to one section)
8. Process ("How It Works" — 3 steps)
9. Service area map
10. Footer CTA ("Get Free Estimate" form)

### CTA Patterns
| Pattern | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Primary CTA text | "Get a Free Estimate" / "Get a Free Quote" / "Request Consultation" |
| Secondary CTA | "View Our Portfolio" / "See Our Work" |
| Avoid | "Learn More," "Submit," "Click Here" |
| Color | High-contrast vs. background — orange/yellow on green, white on dark |
| Placement | Hero, after every service description, next to testimonials, sticky header |
| Mobile | Sticky footer bar with click-to-call phone number (80% of leads = phone calls) |

### Conversion Elements
| Element | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Before/after slider | Highest single engagement element; proves transformation |
| Filterable gallery | Lets users self-select relevant project type; reduces bounce |
| Floating Google rating overlay | Persistent social proof without interrupting content |
| 3-step "How It Works" | Demystifies the process; reduces fear of commitment |
| Click-to-call sticky button | Captures the 80% who prefer calling over forms |
| Crew/team photos | Psychological trust; homeowners are inviting strangers onto property |
| License + insurance badges near forms | Directly counters the "cowboy contractor" fear |
| Local award badges | Brizscapes (Landscape Queensland 2018–20), Lawns & Palms — above the fold |

### Form Design
| Field | Include? |
|-------|---------|
| Name | Yes |
| Phone | Yes |
| Email | Yes |
| Address / City | Yes (enables routing + local SEO data) |
| Service Type (dropdown) | Yes |
| Project description (optional textarea) | Optional — keep it low-friction |
| Budget | No — kills conversions |
| Total fields | Max 5–6 |

## So What? (Implications for Our Templates)

- **Before/after slider is non-negotiable** — include in every landscaping template
- **Sticky header pattern** — logo + phone number + "Get Free Estimate" that follows scroll
- **Trust bar section** — immediately after hero, before services
- **Filterable gallery** — category buttons (Patios, Lawns, Water Features, Hardscaping, Lighting)
- **Service pages not service sections** — template structure needs dedicated sub-pages per service category for SEO
- **CTA color: orange or bright yellow** — highest contrast against green-dominant palette
- **Crew photo mandatory** — at minimum one real team photo in About section
- **3-step process section** — reduces friction and fear for first-time callers
- **Local variant slots** — city name, city-specific hero photo, local review snippets
