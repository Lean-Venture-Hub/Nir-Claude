# Med Spa Go-To-Market Strategy
_Last updated: 2026-03-05_

## TL;DR

Target Segment 1 (Leaky Funnel) in DFW first. They have the budget, the volume problem, and the sophistication to buy. Core offer: a $1,500/mo retention marketing system (automated rebooking + review capture) with a $3,000 setup fee. Cold email + Instagram DM combo. Differentiate on proof, not promises — use dentist results as your credibility wedge. First 3 clients by Day 45. $15K MRR by Day 90.

---

## 1. Target Segment: Segment 1 — Leaky Funnel

**Why S1, not S2:**
- S1 is 73-78% of the market (~3,000+ spas). Abundance means selectivity.
- S1 spas have online booking + reviews + website = they have infrastructure to convert, they're just not retaining.
- They can write a check without CFO approval. S2 spas with <20 reviews often don't have cash flow yet.
- Higher sophistication = shorter sales cycle. They already know they have a problem.
- S2 is a backup funnel: they need proof first, which means longer trust-build.

**Ideal customer profile within S1:**
- Rating 4.3-4.8 (not 5.0 — perfect scores are often gamed and owners are complacent)
- 50-200 reviews (enough volume to work with, not yet maxed out)
- Online booking detected but avg site score ~7.3 (room to improve conversion)
- Fresh reviews (<6 months) = active practice, not dying
- Group practice = multiple providers = more revenue at stake = more pain from churn
- Instagram present but not posting consistently (the most common pattern)

**City priority:** DFW first. You already have the data, it's your backyard, TX physician-ownership means operators are stretched thin and welcome outsourced marketing.

## 2. Core Offer

**Do NOT pitch a full marketing retainer. They've heard it 50 times.**

Pitch a narrow, high-value wedge: **the Retention System**.

### Offer: "Fill Your Empty Chairs" — $1,500/mo + $3,000 setup

**What it does (plain English pitch):**
> "We plug into your booking system and automatically follow up with patients who haven't returned in 60-90 days. We also capture new reviews from your best patients. Most clients see 20-40 rebooking appointments within 60 days."

**What you actually deliver:**
- Automated win-back email/SMS sequence (3-touch, 90-day window)
- Post-visit review request automation (triggered by booking platform)
- Monthly Google Business Profile optimization (photos, posts, Q&A)
- Monthly report: rebookings attributed, review count delta, GBP stats

**Why this specific offer:**
- Measurable ROI: 1 Botox patient rebooked = $400-700. Client pays $1,500/mo. ROI obvious in week 2.
- No dependency on their social content or ad budget — you own the workflow end-to-end
- Integrates with Vagaro/Boulevard/Zenoti via native automations (no custom dev needed)
- Scales: once the playbook is built, each new client costs ~5 hrs/month to run

**Upsell path (Month 3+):**
- SEO add-on: $800/mo (local landing pages, GBP posts, citation cleanup)
- Paid ads management: $750/mo management fee + ad spend
- Full content/social: $1,200/mo

## 3. Outreach Approach

**Kill list (do not pursue):**
- Partnership with booking platforms (too slow, too political)
- SEO-only cold outreach (commoditized, long sales cycle)
- Referral-only (too slow for 90-day goal)
- Paid ads to generate inbound (too expensive before PMF)

**What works:**

### Channel 1: Cold Email (primary — 60% of effort)
- Source: labeled-medspas.csv filtered to S1, Group, Fresh reviews, DFW
- Find owner email via: website contact, LinkedIn, Instagram bio
- Volume: 30 personalized emails/day (not blasted — true 1:1 personalization using their Google reviews and site score)
- Subject line formula: "[Their Spa Name] — noticed X patients haven't rebooked in 60+ days"
- Email body: 4 sentences. One specific observation about their business. One pain point. One result claim from a dentist client. One CTA to a 15-min call.
- Follow-up: 3 touches over 10 days, then mark dead.

### Channel 2: Instagram DM (secondary — 30% of effort)
- Target: spas with Instagram detected, posting 1-3x/week (engaged but not optimized)
- Message: 2 sentences. Compliment a specific recent post. Ask if they're happy with how many first-time clients come back.
- Do NOT pitch in DM. Goal is to get a reply. Then move to email or call.
- Volume: 10-15 DMs/day (IG limits ~50 DMs/day for new accounts)

### Channel 3: Strategic referrals (10% of effort)
- Build relationships with 2-3 Vagaro/Boulevard certified consultants. They already talk to med spa owners about software. You can be their recommended marketing partner.
- One intro call per week, no more.

## 4. Pricing

| Tier | Price | What's Included | Target Client |
|------|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| Setup (one-time) | $3,000 | Automation build, onboarding, integration | All |
| Retention Core | $1,500/mo | Win-back sequences, review automation, GBP | S1 solo/small group |
| Retention Pro | $2,500/mo | Above + SEO local pages + monthly report call | S1 group, multi-location |
| Full Growth | $4,500/mo | Above + social content + paid ads mgmt | Scale phase only |

**Pricing rationale:**
- $1,500/mo is below their mental "marketing agency" threshold ($2K-5K/mo they've been pitched)
- Setup fee creates commitment + covers your onboarding labor
- $3K setup + $1,500 x 3 months = $7,500 in 90 days per client — at 3 clients = $22.5K Q1
- Do NOT discount setup fee. Clients who don't pay setup churn faster.

## 5. Differentiation

**The med spa agency market is crowded. Here's how you don't compete with them:**

| What most med spa agencies pitch | What you pitch |
|----------------------------------|----------------|
| "We'll grow your Instagram" | "We'll bring back patients who already trust you" |
| "Brand awareness campaigns" | "20-40 rebookings in 60 days, measurable" |
| "Full-service digital marketing" | One system, one outcome, one price |
| "We specialize in aesthetics" | "We already do this for dental practices. Health-adjacent, same patient psychology." |

**Your actual moats:**
1. **Data advantage:** You know their review count, site score, booking status before the first email. Use it.
2. **Cross-vertical proof:** Dentist results are directly relevant (same appointment-based, same patient LTV logic). No med spa agency has this.
3. **Operational precision:** You can reference specific platforms (Vagaro, Boulevard, Zenoti) by name. Most agencies are platform-agnostic = generic.
4. **Speed to ROI:** Retention marketing shows results in 30-60 days. SEO takes 6 months. Ads are unpredictable. You win on time-to-value.

## 7. Kill List — Ideas We're Not Pursuing (90 Days)

- Paid ads management as lead offer (requires ad spend, unpredictable ROI story, harder to sell)
- Building a SaaS tool (wrong phase)
- Targeting NYC or Atlanta first (regulatory friction + saturation)
- Targeting S2 or S3 segments (wrong budget, wrong mindset)
- White-label partnerships (distraction until $30K MRR)
- Webinars or content marketing for inbound (too slow for 90-day goal)
