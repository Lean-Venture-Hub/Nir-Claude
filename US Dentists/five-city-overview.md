# Six-City Dental Market Overview

**TL;DR:** 11,847 clinics across 6 metros. 67% (7,917) are Seg 1 — high-reputation practices already online but likely leaking patients. DFW is the largest market (3,928 clinics). Houston has the highest growth + lowest competition. Phoenix has the highest digital maturity. NYC has the most fragmented digital presence. Priority order: Houston > DFW > Phoenix > Charlotte > Atlanta > NYC.

## City-Specific Notes

**DFW (3,928 clinics — LARGEST MARKET)**
- Biggest metro in the dataset by far; 2,712 Seg 1 clinics alone
- High growth market (+198K/yr population), medium agency competition
- 86% have websites, 61% social media — digitally mature
- 307 Seg 4 (no website) = largest absolute count of any city for website-build pitch
- 63 Seg 5 (Digitally Absent) = highest count, tied with Charlotte-level opportunity
- 649 "No reviews" (16%) = massive untapped reputation-building opportunity
- 2,726 Group practices (69%) = large accounts, higher contract value

**Houston (2,389 clinics — START HERE)**
- Highest growth + lowest agency competition = best beachhead market
- Seg 1 dominance: 1,649 clinics (69% of city) — unusually high ICP density
- High social media adoption (64%) = paid social ads are a natural upsell
- 336 unknowns in practice type = data enrichment opportunity before outreach

**Phoenix (1,092 clinics)**
- Highest digital maturity of any market: 92% have websites, 46% online booking
- Lowest Seg 3 (34 clinics) = fewer distressed practices, cleaner book of business
- Strong Seg 1 ratio (77%) — practices here are ready for the next level, not the basics
- Lead with performance marketing + conversion rate optimization pitches

**Charlotte (1,425 clinics)**
- Fastest-growing market (very high growth rate)
- Highest Seg 5 count (28) and large Seg 4 (184) — more digitally lagging than peers
- 196 "No reviews" clinics = untouched territory for reputation-building pitch
- 982 Group practices = larger accounts, higher deal value per client

**Atlanta (1,077 clinics)**
- Smallest market in the set; lowest online booking rate (39%)
- Seg 1 is 65% of market — ICP is there, but digital maturity trails
- 118 "No reviews" = reputation gap angle works here
- Good third-market: let Houston and Phoenix prove the model first

**NYC (1,936 clinics)**
- Lowest website penetration (76%) — 10-16 points below every other city
- Highest Seg 3 count (341) — most reputation-distressed market in the set
- 1,245 Group practices (64%) but also 397 Solo — pitch needs to bifurcate
- High cost of living = higher willingness to pay IF you can show ROI
- Most operationally complex: boroughs create fragmented geographic logic
- Recommend NYC last — hardest market, most noise, most competition
