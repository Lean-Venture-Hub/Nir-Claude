"Dental Implants Changed My Life" — Noa's Story from Jackson Heights, NY

Richard P Medina DDS March 2, 2026 5 min read

Noa Cohen, 34, from Jackson Heights, NY, came to Richard P Medina after years of avoiding dental care. "My fear of dentists was so great that I simply stopped going," she says. "I knew things were getting worse, but I preferred to ignore it." What started as minor neglect turned into a reality that affected every area of her life — from work to personal relationships.

When Fear Became Reality

Like many others, Noa avoided dental visits for years. What started as treatment anxiety gradually became a complete cycle of avoidance. Her front teeth began to deteriorate, and instead of seeking help, she found ways to hide the problem.

In photos, she always kept her mouth closed. In work meetings, she felt insecure. At dinners with friends, she avoided certain foods. Gradually, the physical problem became an emotional one that affected every aspect of her life.

"I reached a point where I stopped smiling. In family photos, I always closed my mouth. It affected my self-confidence at work and in my personal life."

Noa admits it took her a while to understand that the situation wouldn't improve on its own. "Your body talks to you," she says, "and when I started feeling severe pain, I realized I had no choice."

The First Visit Richard P Medina

The change came through a close friend who told Noa about a positive experience at Richard P Medina. "She told me it was a completely different clinic from what I knew," Noa recalls. "They listen, don't judge, and explain every step."

."

During the initial consultation, an advanced CT scan was performed that allowed Dr. Medina to build a precise treatment plan. Every step was explained in simple language, including timelines, costs, and what to expect at each stage. "For the first time, I felt like I understood what was going to happen," says Noa. "That alone reduced my fear."

The Treatment Process — Step by Step

Noa's treatment plan included two front tooth implants. The process took several months, with each step tailored to her pace:

  1. Initial consultation and planning — Noa felt completely comfortable. "There was no pressure, no surprises. Everything was transparent and clear."
  2. Preparation — Bone graft — One of the implants required a preliminary bone graft. "I was worried about this step, but Dr. Medina explained that it's a routine procedure that ensures a stable result for years to come."
  3. The implant itself — "It was much less painful than I expected. Dr. Medina worked with precision and gentleness, and thanks to the local anesthesia, I barely felt anything."
  4. Recovery period — Our team accompanied Noa with follow-up calls and regular checkups. "I felt like someone was taking care of me. Every small question got an immediate answer."
  5. The final crown — "When I saw the final result, I burst into tears. That was the moment I realized every minute of waiting was worth it."
"When I saw the final result in the mirror, I burst into tears. It was the first time in years that I smiled without thinking twice."

Life After Implants

Today, six months after completing treatment, Noa describes a fundamental change in her quality of life. "I smile in photos. I eat whatever I want. I walk into work meetings with a confidence I never knew." The new teeth look completely natural, and no one around her can tell they are implants.

"This investment was one of the best decisions I've ever made," she summarizes. "If you're also hesitating — don't wait like I did. There are solutions, and there are people who know how to help."

Note: Results may vary from patient to patient. The story presented is based on a personal experience and does not constitute a guarantee of similar results. Each treatment is personalized following professional diagnosis.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are dental implants suitable for everyone?
Most healthy adults are suitable candidates for dental implants. At the initial consultation, we check bone density, gum health, and general medical history. Even in cases of bone resorption, a preliminary bone graft can enable successful implantation. It's important to consult with a specialist for a personal assessment.
How soon after the implant can I return to normal activity?
Most patients return to normal activity within a day or two after implantation. We recommend soft food for about a week after surgery. Full recovery — the integration of the implant with the jawbone — takes 3 to 6 months, and during this time patients continue with a completely normal lifestyle.
