# Segment 1 Outreach Strategy — Leaky Funnel Clinics

**TL;DR:** Seg 1 clinics aren't in pain — you have to manufacture urgency by showing them a specific, quantified leak they didn't know existed. Lead with data, not services. The best sub-segments are those where the gap between their reputation and their digital infrastructure is widest and most visible. Priority order: 1A → 1B → 1C → 1D.

---

## The Core Insight

Seg 1 clinics suffer from **complacency blindness**. They're busy, reviews are decent, things feel fine. The only message that cuts through is one that says: *"Here's exactly how many patients you're losing, and here's the proof."* Generic "grow your practice" pitches will be ignored.

Your data gives you the ammunition. Use it as a diagnostic, not a pitch.

---

## Sub-Segment Priority Matrix

| Sub-Seg | Definition | Est. % of Seg 1 | Pain Point | Why They'll Buy |
|---------|------------|-----------------|------------|-----------------|
| **1A** | No online booking + site score < 6 + Group practice | ~15% | Patients find them, can't convert | High patient volume = high revenue loss per conversion gap |
| **1B** | No social media + review recency Aging/Stale + Fresh reviews stopped | ~20% | Losing momentum, won't notice until it's too late | Fear of slipping — they've seen peers decline |
| **1C** | Has booking + site score < 5 + no social | ~18% | Booking exists but funnel still leaks pre-click | Can be shown a specific audit failure, not a vague gap |
| **1D** | All digital boxes checked, site score 7+ | ~12% | Nothing obvious — wrong timing | Do not prioritize. Low urgency, high resistance. |

---

## Sub-Segment Playbooks

### 1A — "The Invisible Wall" (HIGHEST PRIORITY)
**Data signals:** `has_online_booking = No` + `site_score < 6` + `practice_type = Group`

**The insight:** Group practices with 4+ stars and 20+ reviews are generating demand — then losing it at the booking step. At 3-5 dentists seeing 15 patients/day, even a 5% conversion improvement = $150K+/yr.

**Value prop:** "You're showing up in Google. People are clicking. They're not booking. We can show you exactly where they leave."

**Outreach angle:** Lead with a free website audit framed as a conversion gap analysis, not a design critique. Quantify the loss ("practices like yours lose 8-12 new patients/month at this step").

**Channel:** Email → personalized with their clinic name, site score, and no-booking callout. No generic templates.

**CTA:** "Can I send you a 5-minute audit of [Clinic Name]'s website?" — not "book a call."

---

### 1B — "The Fading Star" (HIGH PRIORITY)
**Data signals:** `review_recency = Aging or Stale` + `instagram_url = null` + `facebook_url = null`

**The insight:** Their reputation peaked 18-24 months ago. New competitors with active social and fresh reviews are slowly stealing their Google ranking and word-of-mouth. They don't feel it yet.

**Value prop:** "Your reviews are aging out. Google's algorithm deprioritizes clinics with old review activity. Here's where you stand vs. new practices in [area]."

**Outreach angle:** Competitive intelligence frame. Show them a local competitor with 80 reviews in the last 6 months vs. their last review being 14 months ago. Make it visual and specific.

**Channel:** Cold email + LinkedIn DM to owner/office manager. For solo practices, try direct call during non-peak hours (Tue-Thu, 11am-1pm).

**CTA:** "Want to see how your review velocity compares to the top 3 practices in [neighborhood]?"

---

### 1C — "The Broken Bridge" (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
**Data signals:** `has_online_booking = Yes` + `site_score < 5` + no social media present

**The insight:** They invested in booking but the website experience is weak enough to undermine trust before conversion. They think they've "solved digital" because booking exists — they haven't.

**Value prop:** "You have the booking button — but 60% of visitors never reach it. Here's what's stopping them."

**Outreach angle:** Run a real audit using their site score data points. Reference specific issues (slow load, no trust signals, buried CTA). Frame it as "you're halfway there, not broken."

**Channel:** Email with a loom-style personalized video walkthrough of their website (2 min). Highest response rates for this segment.

**CTA:** "Here's a 2-min video of what I found on your site. Happy to send the full audit free."

---

### 1D — "The Satisfied Patient" (DEPRIORITIZE)
**Data signals:** `has_online_booking = Yes` + `site_score >= 7` + active social + `review_recency = Fresh`

**Decision:** Do not outreach now. No visible gap = no urgency = long sales cycle.

**Exception:** If you have a referral or warm intro, take it. Otherwise, skip to next quarter.

---

## Sequencing & Volume Recommendations

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1-2 | Build 1A list. Filter CSV: `has_online_booking=No`, `site_score<6`, `practice_type=Group`. Run manual audit on top 20 by review count. |
| 3-4 | Deploy 1A outreach. Target 50 clinics/week. Personalize first email with clinic name + specific site score + local market reference. |
| 5-6 | Add 1B list. Layer in competitive review data. A/B test "fading star" subject lines. |
| 7-8 | 1C outreach with Loom videos. Cap at 20/week — time-intensive but highest show rate. |
| 9+ | Review reply rates. Kill bottom 50% of messaging angles. Double down on what converts. |

---

## What to Kill Now

- Generic "grow your practice" cold emails — 0% open rate with Seg 1
- Outreach to 1D (satisfied) — not worth the effort this quarter
- Social media-only pitches without a conversion gap story — too abstract
- Any messaging that leads with YOUR agency capabilities vs. THEIR specific data

---

## Key Metrics to Track

- Email open rate by sub-segment (target: >35% for 1A with personalization)
- Reply rate (target: >8%)
- Discovery call booking rate (target: >3% of total outreach)
- Sub-segment conversion rate — to identify which slice actually closes
