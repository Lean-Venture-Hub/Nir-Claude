# Seg 1A Pitch Playbook — Group Practice, Good Reputation, No Booking

**TL;DR:** They have a leaky funnel. People look them up, see a mediocre site with no booking button, and go to the next result. You are selling one thing: a system that converts their existing reputation into booked appointments — automatically, without adding staff. Show them their own leaky funnel in 5 minutes. Close on a fixed monthly retainer with a per-new-patient guarantee.

---

## 1. What to Prepare Before the Meeting

Pull from their public data and build this before you walk in:

| Prep Item | How to Get It | What It Proves |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Screenshot of their Google listing | Google Maps URL | Anchor for the conversation |
| Competitor map (3-5 nearby practices) | Google Maps search | Their rank vs. nearby competitors |
| "Patient journey" screenshare path | Their website URL | Where patients drop off |
| Review velocity calculation | review_count + latest_review_age | Are they collecting reviews or coasting? |
| Missing booking friction screenshot | Site score <6, no booking | The exact dollar leak |
| Facebook/Instagram audit | facebook_url, instagram_url | Ghost accounts = missed trust signals |

Print a 1-page "Practice Audit" PDF: their name, their numbers, their gaps. Hand it to them. Do not email it in advance.

---

## 2. The "Holy Shit" Moment — First 5 Minutes

**The Leaky Chair Demo.**

Say this: "You have [X] Google reviews averaging [Y] stars. That means roughly [X * 3] people read your reviews last month. Let me show you what they saw when they clicked your website."

Then open their site on a laptop. Do this live:
1. Show the homepage — note the load time, the generic stock photo, the missing "Book Now" button above the fold.
2. Hit the phone number. Does it auto-dial on mobile? (Usually no.)
3. Try to book an appointment. Count the clicks and friction.
4. Pull up a competitor with online booking. Show the difference in 10 seconds.

Then say: "Every person who landed here and couldn't book in 30 seconds went here instead." Open the top competitor.

Finish with the number: "Based on your review traffic, I estimate you're losing 8-15 new patient inquiries per month to friction. At $5,000 per patient lifetime value, that's $40K-$75K a year walking out the door."

They will ask: "Can you fix that?" That is your close.

---

## 3. The Exact Product You Are Selling

**The New Patient Machine** (name it, it closes faster)

Three-part system, delivered in 30 days:

**Part 1 — Convert (Week 1-2)**
- Rebuild or refront their website with a mobile-first, above-the-fold booking CTA
- Integrate real online booking (Zocdoc, NexHealth, or their existing PMS)
- Fix click-to-call, Google Business Profile sync, hours accuracy

**Part 2 — Capture (Week 2-3)**
- Automated post-visit review request sequence (SMS + email, HIPAA-compliant)
- Review velocity target: 4+ new reviews/month minimum
- Social proof widgets on site (live Google rating pull)

**Part 3 — Retain (Ongoing)**
- Monthly reactivation campaign to lapsed patients (no-show or 18+ months since visit)
- Quarterly performance report: new patients from organic, new reviews added, site traffic
- 1 Instagram/Facebook post per week using before/after cases or patient education (they provide photo/approval, you build and post)

What you do NOT sell them: ads, SEO campaigns, logo redesigns, brand strategy. Not yet. This is the foundation.

---

## 4. Pricing Model

**Two-tier, no hourly, no project fees.**

| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Setup Fee (one-time) | $2,500 | Website rebuild/refont, booking integration, review system setup |
| Monthly Retainer | $1,200/mo | Review capture automation, social posts (4/mo), reactivation campaigns, monthly report |

**Performance kicker (optional, closes fence-sitters):**
Add a per-new-patient-attributed fee of $150 if they want to pay lower retainer. Track with UTM links + booking source tagging. If they prefer certainty, keep the flat $1,200.

**Minimum commitment:** 3 months. After that, month-to-month.

**Why this works:** $1,200/mo feels like nothing against a $5,000+ LTV patient. They need less than 1 new patient every 4 months to break even. You will deliver 5-15/month.

---

## 5. Making the ROI Undeniable

Do the math out loud, in front of them, on paper.

```
Monthly site visitors (estimate from review count proxy): ~300
Current conversion to booking (no online booking, slow site): ~1% = 3/mo
Post-system conversion target: 4-5% = 12-15/mo
Incremental new patients: 9-12/mo
Conservative LTV per patient: $5,000
Monthly incremental revenue: $45,000 - $60,000
Your monthly fee: $1,200
ROI: 37x - 50x
```

Then say: "I'm not asking you to gamble. I'm asking you to spend $1,200 to capture revenue you're already generating and currently throwing away."

**The one-liner close:** "You already earned this reputation. You're just not collecting on it."

## 6. Objections and Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We're busy enough" | "Great. Then reactivation campaigns fill chairs when you have openings, not all the time." |
| "We tried marketing before" | "What did you try? Ads? This isn't ads. This converts people already looking for you." |
| "We need to think about it" | "What specifically? The ROI, the timeline, or the fee? Let me address that now." |
| "Our office manager handles this" | "Perfect. She'd love a system that removes tasks from her plate. Can I show her the same demo?" |
