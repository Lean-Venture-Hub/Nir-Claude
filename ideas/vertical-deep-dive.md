# US SMB Website Sales: Vertical Deep Dive
*Date: 2026-03-11 | Based on Census Bureau, BrightLocal, IBISWorld, competitor pricing data*

## TL;DR Recommendation

**Start with Auto Repair OR Landscaping.**

- **Auto Repair** is the play if you want raw scale: 247,500 businesses, 50-55% have reviews but no website, clear booking/review upsell, high pain intensity (missed calls = lost revenue). Biggest risk: ServiceTitan is closing in.
- **Landscaping** is the play if you want best unit economics: $1,500-3,500 LTV, 50-60% upsell to local ads management, owners easy to reach, seasonal urgency = easier close. 152,000 businesses.
- **Avoid food trucks** (LTV broken), **avoid HVAC/plumbing** (ServiceTitan dominates, harder to break in).
- **Nail salons and barbershops** are volume plays only — huge gap, fast close, but low LTV ($600-1,600) and moderate churn. Works if you can automate setup to <2 hours per site.

## Notes Per Vertical

**Auto Repair** — Largest raw market (247,500 shops). Owners are on-site, answerable, 70-80% pick up phones. Clear ROI story: missed appointment calls = lost $300+ jobs. ServiceTitan ($200/mo) is the threat — you need faster setup or better local SEO as positioning. Upsell to review management + Google Local Services Ads is natural.

**Landscaping** — Best overall unit economics. 85% are 1-5 employee shops; owners carry phones and are reachable early AM or 5-6 PM. Before/after gallery drives instant ROI. 50-60% will add local ads management (+$500-2,000/mo) making this the highest upsell rate vertical. Seasonal urgency (spring surge) creates natural close windows. Housecall Pro is a threat but less dominant than ServiceTitan in HVAC.

**Nail Salons** — Highest gap (75-80%), extremely templateable (9/10), fastest close. But: 40-50% retention, Vagaro dominates booking, owners often prefer cash/one-time. Works as a **volume play** only — if you can build and launch a site in under 2 hours, you can profit at $400-600 one-time.

**Barbershops** — 70-75% gap, very little competition. Near-zero vertical SaaS here. Quick close (1-2 weeks). Same economics as nail salons: lower LTV ($800-1,600), but extremely templateable (9/10) and owners are approachable. Could bundle nail salons + barbershops as a single "beauty & grooming" template play.

**Cleaning Services** — 168,000 businesses, 60-70% solo operators. Hard to reach (on jobs all day). Strong recurring-revenue potential (booking integration for subscription cleanings). Housecall Pro is strong competitor. Best reached via text/evening calls.

**HVAC/Plumbing** — Highest LTV ($2,000-5,000) and highest pain intensity (9/10 — emergency services need 24/7 web presence). BUT ServiceTitan is fully entrenched at $250-350/mo including website. Only viable if you position as a local SEO + credibility layer that ServiceTitan's generic templates don't provide. High-effort, high-reward.

**Pet Groomers** — Underrated. 65-70% gap, owners accessible (in shop, consistent hours), easy decision. Vagaro is growing here but not dominant. Similar template to nail salons but better LTV ($900-1,800) and pet owner psychology (they research extensively). Decent backup vertical.

**Tattoo Parlors** — Creative owners want design control; hard to sell templates. Smallest market (9,000). Portfolio-first requirement makes scaling harder. Skip as primary.

**Food Trucks** — Avoid as core. LTV ($400-900) is too low, churn is high (many fail within 2 years), and owners don't see websites as a pain point. Instagram + Google Maps is enough for most.

## Recommended Sequencing

1. **Start:** Auto Repair or Landscaping (best LTV, large market, reachable owners)
2. **Layer in volume:** Nail Salons or Barbershops (fast close, high gap, use same lightweight template)
3. **Avoid until scale:** HVAC/Plumbing (fight ServiceTitan only when you have traction + differentiation)
4. **Never:** Food Trucks as a core play

**Upsell stack that works across all verticals:** Website ($500-1,500 one-time) → Review management ($30-50/mo) → Booking integration ($30-50/mo) → Google Local Services Ads management ($300-1,000/mo)
