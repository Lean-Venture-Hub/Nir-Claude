## THE GOLDEN 10

### 1. Auto Repair Shops
- **Volume:** ~280,000 shops in US
- **No-website rate:** ~55% have no site or a parked page
- **Revenue:** $300K–$2M/yr per shop, cash-heavy
- **Google presence:** Excellent — customers leave reviews constantly
- **Scan ease:** "auto repair" is a clean Maps category
- **Competition:** Medium — most agencies ignore them as "unsexy"
- **Why it wins:** Owner is under a car all day. Neglects everything digital. But has money and desperately needs to show up for "mechanic near me" searches. Highest volume + highest neglect combo.

### 2. Nail Salons
- **Volume:** ~56,000 salons in US
- **No-website rate:** ~70% — most run on word of mouth and Yelp
- **Revenue:** $150K–$500K/yr
- **Google presence:** Very high — photos and reviews are core
- **Scan ease:** "nail salon" is exact Maps category, easy to filter
- **Competition:** Low — agencies don't pitch nail salons
- **Why it wins:** Often immigrant-owned, no time, no tech knowledge. Booking and photos are the whole sale. Easy $500–$1,500 close.

### 3. Plumbers (Independent)
- **Volume:** ~120,000 independent plumbing businesses
- **No-website rate:** ~60%
- **Revenue:** $400K–$3M/yr — high ticket jobs
- **Google presence:** Strong, "emergency plumber" searches are massive
- **Scan ease:** "plumber" category is clean on Maps
- **Competition:** Medium — some franchise-focused agencies exist
- **Why it wins:** One good website + Google Business setup = $10K+ in extra jobs monthly. High willingness to pay because ROI is obvious.

### 4. Electricians (Independent)
- **Volume:** ~95,000 independent electrical contractors
- **No-website rate:** ~58%
- **Revenue:** $500K–$5M/yr — premium pricing
- **Google presence:** Strong
- **Scan ease:** "electrician" is a clean category
- **Competition:** Medium
- **Why it wins:** Same profile as plumbers. High job value = high willingness to invest in leads. One electrical panel job = $2,000+.

### 5. Barbershops
- **Volume:** ~80,000 in US
- **No-website rate:** ~65% — most use Instagram or nothing
- **Revenue:** $100K–$400K/yr
- **Google presence:** Very high — people check hours, photos, reviews
- **Scan ease:** "barber shop" is a clean Maps category
- **Competition:** Very low — no one is building barbershop websites at scale
- **Why it wins:** Culturally, barbers are community pillars. Pride in their shop. Easy sell on a booking page + photo gallery. Average deal: $400–$800.

### 6. Landscaping / Lawn Care
- **Volume:** ~600,000 businesses (including solo operators)
- **No-website rate:** ~75% — most run on flyers and referrals
- **Revenue:** $100K–$2M/yr depending on commercial work
- **Google presence:** Medium — reviews exist but less frequent
- **Scan ease:** "landscaping" and "lawn care service" work on Maps; filter by review count to remove solo ops
- **Competition:** Low — agencies mostly ignore this
- **Why it wins:** Massive volume + very high neglect rate. Easy before/after photo website pitch. Seasonal urgency creates sales windows.

### 7. HVAC (Independent)
- **Volume:** ~110,000 independent HVAC companies
- **No-website rate:** ~52%
- **Revenue:** $500K–$10M/yr — very high ticket
- **Google presence:** Strong, especially in summer/winter emergency searches
- **Scan ease:** "HVAC contractor" works on Maps
- **Competition:** Medium-high — some national SEO agencies target HVAC
- **Why it wins:** Highest revenue potential per client. An HVAC company landing one commercial contract via website = worth $50K+. They'll pay $3,000–$10,000 for a proper site.

### 8. Cleaning Services (Residential)
- **Volume:** ~300,000+ businesses
- **No-website rate:** ~70%
- **Revenue:** $150K–$800K/yr
- **Google presence:** Very strong — people heavily research before letting strangers in
- **Scan ease:** "house cleaning service" / "maid service" work on Maps
- **Competition:** Low
- **Why it wins:** Trust is the sale. A clean, professional website with reviews embedded = direct conversion. Easy pitch. High close rate.

### 9. Roofers (Independent)
- **Volume:** ~100,000 independent roofing companies
- **No-website rate:** ~55%
- **Revenue:** $500K–$5M/yr — very high ticket jobs
- **Google presence:** Strong, especially post-storm surges
- **Scan ease:** "roofing contractor" is reliable on Maps
- **Competition:** Medium — storm-chasing agencies exist but don't scale locally
- **Why it wins:** Single job worth $8,000–$25,000. One new customer from Google pays for 3 years of website costs. Easy ROI pitch.

### 10. Ethnic Restaurants (non-chain)
- **Volume:** ~500,000+ independent restaurants
- **No-website rate:** ~60% — many just have a Facebook page
- **Revenue:** $300K–$1.5M/yr
- **Google presence:** Extremely high — food photos, hours, reviews
- **Scan ease:** Filter by cuisine type on Maps; avoid chains with review count floor (e.g., 20–200 reviews)
- **Competition:** Low at the local/ethnic level
- **Why it wins:** Menu display + online ordering = immediate visible value. "You're losing delivery orders without a site" is a one-sentence pitch.

## Scan Strategy Notes

**Google Maps query format that works:**
- Search: `[category] in [city]`
- Filter: businesses with 5–100 reviews (active but small), no website link in profile, or website = Facebook URL
- API fields to pull: `name`, `website`, `rating`, `user_ratings_total`, `formatted_phone_number`, `formatted_address`

**No-website detection signals:**
1. `website` field is empty in Places API
2. `website` points to Facebook, Yelp, or is a GoDaddy parked page
3. Website URL returns 404 or hasn't been updated (check via HTTP headers or Playwright)

**Batch scan order recommendation:**
1. Auto Repair (highest volume + easiest to scan)
2. Nail Salons (cleanest category, highest close rate)
3. Landscaping (massive volume, peak season urgency)
4. Cleaning Services (trust-driven = website is obvious sale)
5. Barbershops (community angle, low competition)
